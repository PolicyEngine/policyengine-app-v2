South Carolina's [House Bill 3492](https://www.scstatehouse.gov/sess126_2025-2026/prever/3492_20241205.htm) proposes to alter the state's Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC). Currently, South Carolina offers a 125% nonrefundable match of the federal EITC, the highest state EITC match in the nation on paper. However, because the credit is entirely nonrefundable, low-income filers with little or no state tax liability receive less actual benefit than appears. H.3492 would make 25% of the excess credit (the portion exceeding tax liability) refundable.

We at PolicyEngine have analyzed the effects of this proposed change on South Carolina and its residents.

Key results for 2026:

- Costs the state $403 million
- Benefits 23.3% of South Carolina residents
- Reduces poverty by 2.1%
- Lowers the Gini index of income inequality by 0.41%

## The reform

South Carolina's current EITC structure illustrates a common limitation of nonrefundable tax credits. While the state nominally matches 125% of the federal EITC, most recipients see only a fraction of this benefit because their state tax liability is too low to absorb the full credit amount.

H.3492 addresses this by making 25% of the excess credit refundable. This means that when a filer's 125% state EITC exceeds their state income tax liability, they would receive 25% of that excess as a refund rather than losing it entirely.

The mathematics reveal why this reform carries a substantial price tag: 25% of the 125% match equals 31.25% of the federal EITC. ## Conclusion

H.3492 would transform South Carolina's state EITC from a largely symbolic benefit into meaningful support for low-income working families. The reform effectively unlocks the "dead" credit value that currently exceeds most recipients' tax liability, converting paper generosity into actual household income.
